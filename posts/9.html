# Top Trending Videos (US)

This year’s top trending videos saw creators convert some of their most popular content into longer running phenomena. By producing sequels in response to audience demand, these creators showed how to turn one-off wins into full-on series with fresh influence: Dream made speedrunning (opens new window) a spectator sport with his increasingly innovative attempts to defeat Minecraft’s Ender Dragon; Mark Rober went from busting package thieves with his homemade glitterbomb (opens new window) to catching phone scammers; and Dude Perfect used their humorous stereotypes (opens new window) format to shine a light on the types of people you’re likely to meet at a game night.

Also making the year’s top trending list: A new survival challenge from MrBeast (opens new window), the Weeknd’s much memeified performance at the Super Bowl Halftime Show (opens new window), a hot take on one of the most talked-about games (opens new window) of the year, a scripted morality lesson (opens new window), a moving musical audition (opens new window), and a fight against zombies (opens new window). Oh, and the socially-distanced inauguration (opens new window) of a new U.S. President and VP.

Watchtime of the top trending videos was even bigger than last year’s: Collectively, these 10 videos were viewed more than 485 million times and for around 70 million hours. The channels behind them total more than 238 million subscribers.
