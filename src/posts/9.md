# 2021’s top trending videos and creators

> <https://blog.youtube/culture-and-trends/2021s-top-trending-videos-and-creators/>

## Top Trending Videos (US)

This year’s top trending videos saw creators convert some of their most popular content into longer running phenomena. By producing sequels in response to audience demand, these creators showed how to turn one-off wins into full-on series with fresh influence: Dream made [speedrunning](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RJ0jdO5ZfU4) a spectator sport with his increasingly innovative attempts to defeat Minecraft’s Ender Dragon; Mark Rober went from busting package thieves with his homemade [glitterbomb](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VrKW58MS12g) to catching phone scammers; and Dude Perfect used their humorous [stereotypes](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EsVPUpDyGec) format to shine a light on the types of people you’re likely to meet at a game night.

Also making the year’s top trending list: A new survival challenge from [MrBeast](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9bqk6ZUsKyAhttps://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9bqk6ZUsKyA), the Weeknd’s much memeified performance at the [Super Bowl Halftime Show](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9rhadTURsrw), a hot take on one of the most [talked-about games](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=06NiFBgT3bA) of the year, a scripted [morality lesson](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uv2vqK8luyY), a moving [musical audition](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CZJvBfoHDk0), and a fight against [zombies](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ChaVhbMyd4w). Oh, and the socially-distanced [inauguration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C-qYgs_yOXA) of a new U.S. President and VP.

Watchtime of the top trending videos was even bigger than last year’s: Collectively, these 10 videos were viewed more than 485 million times and for around 70 million hours. The channels behind them total more than 238 million subscribers.
